IMPORTANT NOTES

⚠ Vibration Sensor — 3.3V ONLY. This sensor runs on 3.3V. Connecting to 5V will permanently damage it. Use the 3.3V pin on the MEGA, not the 5V rail.
⚠ LEDs need resistors. Place a 220Ω resistor between each digital pin and the LED anode. No resistor = burned LED within seconds.
ℹ LCD blank after upload? The I2C address is either 0x27 or 0x3F depending on the module. If screen stays blank, change line 1 of setup() in the code from 0x27 to 0x3F.
ℹ DHT11 orientation. Flat face toward you. Left pin = 5V, middle = signal (Pin 7), right = GND. Reading updates every 2 seconds — this is normal for DHT11.
✅ Startup sequence. Green LED turns on → LCD shows "ROBOT MONITOR / Learning..." → waits 10 seconds to learn baseline → LCD shows "READY" → monitoring begins. Open Serial Monitor at 9600 baud to see live data stream.
✅ Power. Use the USB cable from the Elegoo kit plugged into your laptop. The MEGA powers all components from USB. No external power needed for this setup.

HOW TO DEMO EACH FAILURE MODE

⚙ Motor Overload

1
Let baseline lock (10 sec after startup)
2
Slowly turn potentiometer clockwise
3
Amber LED fires at 65% — warning
4
Red LED + buzzer at 80% — fault
5
LCD shows: FAULT: MOTOR:OVERLOAD

🌡 Motor Overheat

1
Pinch the DHT11 between your fingers
2
Body heat raises temperature reading
3
Amber at 35°C — warning
4
Red at 40°C — fault
5
LCD shows: FAULT: MOTOR:OVERHEAT

📉 Motor Stall

1
Turn pot to ~40% (moderate load)
2
Cover the IR sensor with your finger
3
No rotation pulse for 1.5 seconds
4
System detects stall condition
5
LCD shows: FAULT: MOTOR:STALL

〰 Gear Vibration

1
Turn pot to ~50% (load above 40%)
2
Tap vibration sensor rapidly 5+ times
3
5 pulses within 2 seconds = fault
4
LCD shows: FAULT: GEAR:VIBRATION
5
Represents gear grinding under load
